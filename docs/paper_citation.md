## Plain Language Summary

This study developed machine learning models using real-world specialty pharmacy data to identify patients with rheumatoid arthritis (RA) who are at high risk of early discontinuation of adalimumab due to lack or loss of efficacy. Early identification enables pharmacists to intervene proactively, improving patient outcomes and reducing medication waste.

---

## Key Findings

- Elastic Net achieved the best performance on the test set  
  - **AUC-ROC: 0.886**  
  - **F1 Score: 0.741**  

- Other strong models:
  - Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA)  
  - Support Vector Machine (SVM)  

- Predictive features included:
  - Pain score  
  - Joint swelling  
  - Morning stiffness  
  - Infection history  
  - Comorbidities  

---

## Study Highlights

- Dataset: 300 RA patients from specialty pharmacy data  
- Outcome: Early discontinuation within 6 months due to inefficacy  
- Models evaluated:
  - Logistic Regression  
  - Elastic Net  
  - Random Forest  
  - KNN  
  - SVM  
  - XGBoost  

---

## Clinical Impact

This work demonstrates that routinely collected specialty pharmacy data can be leveraged to:
- Identify high-risk patients early  
- Enable targeted pharmacist intervention  
- Improve therapy outcomes and resource utilization
